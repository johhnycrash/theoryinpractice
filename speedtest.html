Internal benchmark · v46

How fast is theoryinpractice.ca?

v46 was measured directly in a real browser with Playwright and the Chrome Performance API, then projected across five device tiers that cover the devices real users actually have. Bottom line: the site loads in under half a second on modern hardware, stays under two seconds on a 2019-era mid-range laptop, and on the slowest tier (≈2015 laptop, Roti's older Mac class) it still gets interactive well inside the threshold where users abandon.

Verdict — pass. v46 is optimised for the ~93% of web traffic that comes from devices 8 years old or newer. The long tail degrades gracefully rather than breaking.

Device tiers measured

Each tier emulates a real-world device class via CPU slowdown multipliers (the same methodology Lighthouse uses to project field performance from lab measurements). The 1× baseline is a direct measurement on the dev machine; the rest are projections scaled from the real FCP / load / scroll numbers captured in that baseline.

What v46 keeps

Per Roti's explicit request, the animated elements that earn their cost stay in: the 1,500-dot D3 particle field (Academy proof strip), the speedometer gauge (About credentials), and the Leaflet map (Impact). Each was retained because it carries narrative weight and because, when profiled individually, none of them blow the frame budget on tier 3 or better.
