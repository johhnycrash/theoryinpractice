## CONSULTING

Every organisation brings a different problem. We begin with a question — and follow a disciplined four-step process from diagnosis to decision. The process is iterative: after Debrief, clients return to Discover with a sharper question.

### 01 · Discover — Why
What is the real problem? Before any solution is proposed, we establish a precise definition of the problem or opportunity — grounded in evidence, not assumption.

**Output:** A written definition that all stakeholders agree on.

### 02 · Design — How
What does the right solution look like? Your context — your team, your constraints, your dynamics — shapes the approach. We apply Design Thinking to find the best path forward.

**Output:** A solution design grounded in your specific situation.

### 03 · Deliver — What
How do we execute with precision? We implement alongside your team — building systems, developing capability, and measuring results against the objectives defined in Discover.

**Output:** Measurable results. A team that can sustain them.

### 04 · Debrief — What next
What did we learn? We compare results to intent, surface lessons, transfer knowledge, then decide: exit with confidence — or return to Discover with a sharper question.

**Output:** Documented lessons. A clear decision on next steps.

### In Practice · Global Engineering Firm · Project Controls

**Discover**
The stated problem: project controllers needed help with Earned Value Management. The real problem, uncovered through diagnosis: the tools existed, the templates existed. What was missing was a shared understanding of why disciplined planning matters. Not technical. Behavioural.

**Design**
Because the problem was a why problem, the solution led with theory, not tools. A scenario-based workshop built on one principle: without a clear Work Breakdown Structure, no forecasting system works. No new tools introduced — the existing ones were made usable.

**Deliver**
Three sessions in one week — Eastern, Central, and Pacific time zones. Teams worked on their actual projects, actual WBS gaps, actual forecasting challenges.

93.3% would recommend this program.

**Debrief**
Two subsequent sessions followed. Session two: how to build trust between Project Controllers and Project Managers across distributed teams. Session three: how to make the value of Project Controls visible to the people it serves. The client returned to Discover.
