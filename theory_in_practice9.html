Consulting · Hand

We fish with you.

The foundation of strategy is good theory. Consulting is where that theory meets your specific problem or opportunity — through a disciplined process that begins with diagnosis and ends with a decision.

Book a discovery call →
Discover · Why
What is the real problem?
Every engagement begins with a disciplined diagnosis.
+

Before any solution is proposed, we establish a precise definition of the problem or opportunity — grounded in theory, not assumption.

Output
A written definition of the problem or opportunity that all stakeholders agree on.
In practice

A global engineering firm approached us through our university partnership. The stated problem: their project controllers needed help with Earned Value Management. The discovery revealed something different. The tools existed. The templates existed. What was missing was a shared understanding of why disciplined planning matters. The real problem was not technical. It was behavioural.

Design · How
What does the right solution look like?
With the problem defined, we design the solution.
+

This is where your context — your team, your constraints, your organisation's specific dynamics — shapes the approach. Using Design Thinking principles, we identify the best path forward from the options available, not from a pre-packaged playbook.

Output
A solution design with clear rationale, grounded in your specific situation.
In practice

Because the problem was a why problem, the solution had to lead with theory, not tools. The design was a scenario-based workshop built around one governing principle: without a clear Work Breakdown Structure, no forecasting system produces reliable results. The workshop would not introduce new tools. It would build the conceptual foundation that made the existing tools usable.

Deliver · What
How do we execute with precision?
Delivery is where theory becomes practice.
+

We implement the solution alongside your team — building the systems, developing the capability, and measuring results against the objectives defined in Discover. The measure of success is not completion. It is outcome.

Output
Measurable results. A functioning system. A team that can sustain it.
In practice

Three sessions in a single week — covering Eastern, Central, and Pacific time zones. Twenty to twenty-five project controllers per session, working in teams on their actual projects, their actual WBS gaps, their actual forecasting challenges.

93.3% of participants would recommend this programme
Debrief · What next
What did we learn, and what comes next?
Most engagements end at delivery. This one does not.
+

The Debrief compares what was achieved against what was intended, surfaces the lessons, and transfers the knowledge to your team. Then a decision: exit with confidence, or return to Discover with a sharper question.

Output
Documented lessons. Knowledge transferred. A clear decision on next steps.
In practice

The engagement did not end at delivery. Two subsequent sessions followed — each surfacing a sharper question than the one before. The first asked why EVM matters. The second asked how to build trust between Project Controllers and Project Managers across distributed teams. The third asked how to make the value of Project Controls visible to the people it serves. The client did not exit. They returned to Discover.
